How Real Products Use This Framework

Every successful product team tracks a different slice of this tree. Here's how five well-known products map their key metrics to the same pillars.

🎵
Spotify
Audio Streaming · B2C SaaS
North Star Monthly Active Streamers (time spent listening)
Growth Free-to-Premium conversion rate, new subscriber CAC
Activation First playlist created, Discover Weekly opened
Retention D30 active streamer rate, Premium renewal rate
Engagement DAU/MAU stickiness, avg session duration, podcasts/week
Revenue MRR from Premium subs, ad RPM for free tier

Spotify places Monthly Active Streamers at the top because listening time is the clearest signal of value delivered. The tree flows top-down: paid and organic Growth feeds new users; Activation fires when a user creates their first playlist or opens Discover Weekly — signaling the algorithm has connected them with music they love. Retention is driven by personalization quality: users who feel “understood” by recommendations renew at significantly higher rates. Engagement (DAU/MAU) determines ad-tier revenue, while Premium MRR grows when listening becomes an indispensable daily habit. Notably, podcast listeners show 2× higher retention than music-only users — a single Engagement metric cascading into Revenue.

Airbnb
Travel Marketplace · Two-sided
North Star Nights Booked (gross booking value)
Growth New host sign-ups, SEO-driven traveler traffic, sign-up CVR
Activation First booking completed, host first listing published
Retention Guest 90-day re-booking rate, host annual listing renewal
Revenue GBV, take rate (~15%), Revenue per Night Booked
Satisfaction Host/guest NPS, review response rate, dispute rate

Nights Booked sits at the intersection of a two-sided marketplace: it only grows if both supply (hosts listing quality properties) and demand (guests finding and trusting the platform) are healthy simultaneously. The metrics tree therefore branches into host-side and guest-side at the second level. The critical insight is that Quality metrics feed directly into Revenue: hosts with higher review scores appear higher in search, get more bookings, and earn more. Airbnb’s data science team standardized all product tradeoff decisions by computing each change’s long-term impact on Nights Booked — a process described in their engineering blog.

Slack
B2B Collaboration SaaS
North Star Messages sent per connected user per week
Growth Workspace sign-ups, K-factor (invites sent per workspace)
Activation “Aha moment”: 2,000 messages sent in a workspace
Retention Monthly workspace retention, seat expansion within accounts
Engagement DAU/MAU, threads/user, Huddle & app integration usage
Revenue Paid seat conversion %, Expansion MRR, Net Revenue Retention

Slack discovered through cohort analysis that workspaces sending 2,000 messages were retained at dramatically higher rates — this “magic number” became their activation threshold. Their metrics tree has an unusually strong viral loop: each user invited to a workspace is simultaneously a Growth and Activation event, making K-factor a leading indicator tracked weekly. For Revenue, Slack tracks seat expansion within accounts as the primary signal that the product is spreading organically inside organizations — a metric that sits uniquely between Retention and Revenue in their tree. Net Revenue Retention above 130% is how they demonstrate that Expansion MRR outpaces churn.

Duolingo
Consumer EdTech · Freemium
North Star Daily Active Learners (DAU)
Growth App install CVR, referral sign-ups, social loop reach
Activation Day-3 streak started (first habit signal), first crown earned
Retention D1/D7/D30 retention, streak continuation & recovery rate
Engagement Lessons/day, avg streak length, league competition participation
Revenue Super Duolingo CVR, subscription LTV, in-app purchase rate

Duolingo’s growth team rebuilt their entire product model around DAU as the North Star, after discovering that streak maintenance was the single biggest driver. When they introduced Streak Freeze (which preserves a streak if a user misses a day), DAU rose significantly without any new content. Their tree shows a tight feedback loop: Engagement (streak length) → Retention (D7/D30) → DAU. For Revenue, they found that users with streaks longer than 10 days convert to Super Duolingo at 3× the rate of new users — a textbook example of how a single Engagement metric becomes a direct Revenue lever. The Duolingo blog published a detailed breakdown of this model.

Figma
B2B Design SaaS · PLG
North Star Weekly collaborative editing sessions
Growth Organic sign-ups from shared file links, education plan CVR
Activation First collaborative edit with another user (PQL signal)
Retention D30 returning editors, file-sharing frequency per user
Engagement Avg editor time/week, prototype views, comments per file
Revenue Team plan conversion %, seats per org, Net Revenue Retention

Figma’s PLG (product-led growth) model makes collaboration the growth engine itself. Every shared file link is a free acquisition channel — the recipient must sign up to view or edit, making Growth and Activation tightly coupled. The “aha moment” is the first collaborative editing session with another user: when teams edit together in real time, they experience the full product value and become far more likely to convert to paid. Figma tracks organizations with 2+ active editors as their primary Revenue expansion signal — a unique metric that sits between Engagement and Revenue in their tree. Once a team reaches that threshold, seat expansion is near-inevitable.
